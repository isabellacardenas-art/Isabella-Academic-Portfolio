Hard Power and Injustice: The Taliban's Use of Coercion to Deny Afghan Women Fundamental Rights

Author: Isabella Cárdenas | Date: October 2025 | Area of Study: International Politics, Human Rights Violations, and Authoritarianism

Abstract/Summary:

This analysis examines the Taliban's systematic **hard power** use since 2021, focusing on coercive control (territory, law) to deny Afghan women fundamental rights like education, employment, and freedom of movement (e.g., airport bans). The regime's actions, including arbitrary arrests, dismantle the justice system. It concludes by highlighting the crucial, ongoing resistance of Afghan women.
